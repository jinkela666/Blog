E-waste pollution

The toxic e-waste around our ordinary life, Dec 11, 2019

The number of e-waste generated worldwide in 2014 was nearly 41.8 million tons, of which 3 million tons came from small electronics. Only a small percentage of such a large amount of e-waste is recycled from the formal and legal channels. The small devices are often difficult to deep recycle in the channels. Unless recycled, smartphones will also be crushed and melted with other metal scraps, becoming a material with low very recycling value.

There are many toxic substances hidden in the smartphone components, such as lead, cadmium, chromium, mercury, arsenic, beryllium, antimony, nickel, polyvinyl chloride, brominated flame retardants and other heavy metals and toxic and hazardous substances.
