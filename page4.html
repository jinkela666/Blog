The dangers of Air pollution


A view to the biggest pollution problem that the Earth ever faced, Dec 17, 2019



"The chemical composition of smog is extremely complex."

Haze refers to the emission of pollutants (gases and particles such as CO, SO2, NOx, NH3, PM) from various sources. Under specific conditions of large airflow fields, fine particles are formed through a series of physical and chemical processes, and the atmospheric extinction phenomenon caused by the interaction with water vapor.

"Photo of the Peking's smog"

"Haze has a great impact on climate, environment and human health."

An increase in PM2.5 concentrations may be responsible for the increase in extreme weather events. PM2.5 affects the atmospheric radiation balance, causing the ground to become colder and the atmosphere to become hotter, seriously affecting regional and global climate change, which may exacerbate the regional atmospheric heating effect and increase extreme weather events. PM2.5 refers to the aerodynamic diameter of less than or equal to 2.5 microns of atmospheric particles (aerosols), the scientific name is atmospheric fine particles. The composition of PM2.5 is extremely complex, including almost all the elements in the periodic table, involving more than 30,000 organic and inorganic compounds. Haze contains high humidity, and can directly infect bacteria and viruses. PM2.5, also known as lungable particles, can directly enter the human alveoli or even the blood system, directly leading to cardiovascular diseases and other diseases. PM2.5 has a large specific surface area and is usually enriched with various heavy metal elements and organic pollutants, which are mostly carcinogens and gene-toxic mutagens, causing great harm.

90% of the haze comes from human social activities




 

The solutions

"The solutions for haze."

First of all, it is necessary to control the emission of SO2, NOx, CO, NH3 and VOCs, the major gaseous substances before the formation of PM2.5. Control of SO2 focuses on industry, heating and coal-fired power plants. NOx control focuses on motor vehicles, coal-fired power plants and industry in that order. CO controls the motor vehicle industry and coal-fired power plants, in that order. VOCs should focus on motor vehicles, disorganized (kitchen, dry cleaners, etc.) emissions and industry. Haze control requires public participation. Public behavior is the most important factor to control the haze. First of all, it is the improvement of the overall quality of all citizens, from food and beverage travel to employment to property selection, to minimize pollution emissions. Only everyone in the whole society starts from themselves, starting from the little things, it is possible to ensure that we have clean air to breath.
