The Ozone Layer


Description of one of the most important Atmosphere's layer, Dec 21, 2019



"What is the Ozone layer?"

Ozone layer refers to the stratosphere of the atmosphere where the concentration of ozone is relatively high. This ozone layer is the human survival umbrella. Ozone in the atmosphere strikes diatomic oxygen mainly with ultraviolet light, dividing it into two atoms, each of which then merges with the unsplit oxygen to form ozone. Ozone molecules are unstable, and after ultraviolet radiation is divided into oxygen molecules and oxygen atoms, forming a continuous process of ozone and oxygen circulation, thus producing the ozone layer. Most of the ozone layer in nature is located 20 to 50 kilometers above the ground. Ozone in the ozone layer is mainly produced by ultraviolet light.

"The function of the ozone layer."

The ozone layer in the atmosphere has three main functions. The first is the protection effect, the ozone layer can absorb the ultraviolet light in the sunlight wavelength 306.3nm below, mainly is part of the UV-B and all of the UV-C, to protect the earth's human, animals and plants from the short-wave ultraviolet damage. Only uv-a and a small amount of UV-B can radiate to the ground, and uv-a does much less damage to biological cells than UV-B. So the ozone layer ACTS as an umbrella to protect life on earth. The second is heating. Ozone absorbs ultraviolet light from the sun and converts it into heat to heat the atmosphere. It is the presence of ozone that makes the stratosphere possible. Other planets don't have ozone or oxygen, so there's no stratosphere. The third is the role of greenhouse gases. Ozone is also important in the upper troposphere and the lower stratosphere, at very low temperatures. If this level of ozone is reduced, there is an incentive to cool the ground. Therefore, the height distribution and variation of ozone are extremely important.

"Causes of Ozone layer damage."

Ozone in the atmosphere can react with many substances to be consumed and destroyed. Of all the substances that react with ozone, the simplest and most reactive are those containing carbon, hydrogen, chlorine, and nitrogen, such as nitrous oxide (N2O), water vapor (H2O), carbon tetrachloride (CCl4), methane (CH4), and now the most important chlorofluorocarbons (CFC). These substances are normally stable in the lower atmosphere, but become ozone-depleting substances when activated in the stratosphere by ultraviolet radiation. This reaction depletes ozone in the stratosphere and upsets the ozone balance, leading to an increase in ultraviolet radiation on the ground, causing a series of problems for the earth's ecology and humans.

"Ozone layer destruction effects."

After the ozone layer is a lot of loss and the ability to absorb ultraviolet radiation greatly abate, lead to reaching the earth's surface uv B significantly increased, to human health and ecological environment bring many hazards, has been widely attention mainly to human body health, terrestrial plants, aquatic ecosystems, biochemistry circulation, the material, and tropospheric atmospheric composition and the influence of air quality, etc. The increase of the UV-B segment can obviously induce three kinds of skin diseases. The latest findings, using data from animal studies and human epidemiology, show that a 10 percent drop in ozone levels increases the incidence of nonmalignant skin tumors by 26 percent. Another type of malignant melanoma is a very dangerous skin disease. Scientific studies have also revealed the intrinsic link between uv-b ultraviolet rays and the incidence of malignant melanoma.

 

Possible solutions

"Ozone layer protection strategy."

The danger brought by the ozone reduction has been widely concerned by the international community. In order to protect the ozone layer from destruction and better protect the ecological environment, the international action to protect the ozone layer has lasted for more than 20 years. The international community has adopted the plan for the protection of the ozone layer, the Vienna convention for the protection of the ozone layer and the Montreal protocol on substances that deplete the ozone layer. China signed the London amendment to the protocol in June 1991, and the number of parties to the protocol now stands at 168. In 1994, the 52nd United Nations general assembly decided that September 16 of each year was the international day for the protection of the ozone layer.
